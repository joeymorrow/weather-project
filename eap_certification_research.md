But I don't actually know the message payloads, nor if different vendors (specifically Raptor and Singlewire) use some specific multicast address or payload/header structure.To get your Beacon Dashboard certified for the Emergency Awareness Protocols (EAP) ecosystem, you don't need a single "EAP certification" (which doesn't formally exist as one unified body). Instead, you should aim for CAP Compliance and Strategic Partnerships with established vendors.🛠️ Path to Certification and IntegrationWhile there is no "free" official certification, you can achieve "certified" status through partner programs and open standards at a low cost.1. CAP Compliance (The "Gold Standard")The Common Alerting Protocol (CAP) is an XML-based data format used to exchange public alerts.Cost: Free to implement the technical standard.Why: Most digital signage targets are "certified" by being CAP-Compliant. This allows your dashboard to "listen" to feeds from FEMA's IPAWS or third-party providers like Rave Mobile Safety.Validation: You can self-certify your compliance by testing against public CAP feeds.2. Vendor Partner ProgramsTo be "certified" for specific ecosystems like Raptor Technologies or Singlewire, you must join their developer programs.Raptor Connect: This is their official integration platform. It allows third-party developers to link with Raptor Alert.Singlewire InformaCast: You can apply to become an "InformaCast-compliant" endpoint. This usually requires a technical review to ensure your software correctly handles their proprietary multicast and registration protocols.Display Requirements & "I Love You Guys"The I Love You Guys Foundation does not mandate specific pixel dimensions, but they do mandate "Term of Art" consistency.Heuristics: If you use their Standard Response Protocol (SRP), you must use their specific language: Hold, Secure, Lockdown, Evacuate, Shelter.Visuals: The Foundation provides free digital materials (vector logos, icons, and colors) for non-commercial use.Full-Page vs. Banner: Best practices for K-12 digital signage typically require a full-page takeover. During an active Lockdown, standard content (weather, menus) must be cleared so the alert is "abundantly clear" from across a cafeteria or hallway.📡 Technical Payload BreakdownTo bridge the gap with high-end vendors, you need to support their specific communication methods.Singlewire InformaCastDiscovery: Devices typically use Service Location Protocol (SLP) to find the InformaCast server.Protocol: They often use a proprietary IP Speaker Protocol for registration and RTP Multicast (G.711-ulaw or G.722) for the actual audio/text stream.Message: The payload includes a priority level (1–100). Your software must be configured to prioritize emergency messages over routine signage.Raptor TechnologiesTrigger: Alerts are usually triggered via Webhooks or APIs (Raptor Connect).Payload: When an incident starts, the payload typically includes the IncidentType (matching SRP values) and Location.Partnership: Schools often look for "Raptor Ready" providers.📋 Recommended Next StepsSign an MOU: Download and sign the Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the "I Love You Guys" Foundation to officially use their terminology in your Beacon Dashboard.Add CAP Support: Implement an XML listener that can parse a CAP 1.2 feed.
